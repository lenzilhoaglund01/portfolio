About

A Little About Me

My love for front-end development began when working at Boats Group with LESS files. In order to grow my knowledge and skills, I then enrolled in Udemy classes, which have significantly aided in my understanding of how to best utilize HTML, CSS, and Javascript.

Some could say that my early education degree puts me at a disadvantage when it comes to the development world. Although I can understand where one might get that idea, I believe that it has given me a much better advantage over other front-end developers.

Working with children has helped me understand the simplicity needed in UI/UX development and design. I believe this is something that can't be taught, but needs to be experienced by yourself and through others.

Even though I didn't know about programming or have anyone around me who understood this world, my educational background has not been a hindrance. Instead, it has helped me understand that I enjoy forever being a 'learner'. Having a 'learner's' attitude, I feel this is the best way to mentor those joining a team that I'm a part of, while also helping me stay current with the new technologies that are ever-evolving.

Career Aspirations

I aspire to help women understand what career opportunities would best fit them. At the same time, I hope to become increasingly knowledgeable and help mentor other developers within my team.
